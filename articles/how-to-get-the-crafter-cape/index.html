How to Get the Crafter Cape in Minecraft

A new exclusive cape is coming to Minecraft as a TwitchCon 2026 limited cosmetic. Bedrock players can try it on early for free right now using Bedrock Cosmos, and there are already rumors swirling about what else might be coming alongside it.

What is the Crafter Cape?

The Crafter Cape is an upcoming limited exclusive cape for Minecraft, tied to TwitchCon 2026. It is one of the rarest cosmetics coming to the game this year, available only to those who attend the event or get an early look through Bedrock Cosmos. On top of that, there are rumors floating around that a second TwitchCon cape could be dropping alongside it, which would make this one of the bigger cape releases Minecraft has seen in a while.

Crafter Cape

Bedrock Edition: Try It Early via Bedrock Cosmos

Minecraft Bedrock Edition

Bedrock players can preview the Crafter Cape right now through Bedrock Cosmos, completely free, even before it is officially distributed at TwitchCon. You do not need to attend the event or change any game settings. Just open Bedrock Cosmos and it is ready to try on.

1
Open Bedrock Cosmos: If you don't have it yet, download it from bedrock-cosmos.app.
2
Go to Edit Character: Open the Edit Character section from the Bedrock Cosmos main menu.
3
Open Capes: Navigate to the Capes tab. This is where you will find the Crafter Cape ready to try on, along with any other upcoming capes before they are officially out.

Quick guide on how to Get the Crafter Cape In Minecraft Bedrock using Bedrock Cosmos!

Could a Second TwitchCon Cape Be Coming?

Here is where things get interesting. Rumors are going around that Mojang might be releasing a second TwitchCon cape alongside the Crafter Cape. The rumored name circulating in the community is the Sapphire Cape, though this has not been officially confirmed by Mojang or Microsoft, so take that with a grain of salt for now.

As for the Crafter Cape itself, it sports a warm brown base reminiscent of a crafting table, with a distinctive horseshoe-shaped diamond highlight arching across the center — a nod to the crafting grid icon familiar to every Minecraft player. The pixelated detailing gives it a clean, iconic look that sits right at home in both Java and Bedrock.

If a second cape does drop, you can expect it to show up in Bedrock Cosmos for everyone to try on. That is how things work here: any new capes that release get added to Cosmos so all users can check them out. So whether it ends up being one cape or two, Bedrock Cosmos will have you covered.

The Crafter Cape is shaping up to be one of the most talked-about Minecraft cosmetics of 2026. Whether you are heading to TwitchCon or just want to see what all the hype is about, Bedrock Cosmos lets you try it on right now for free.
